Common Questions

Everything you need to know

Is this just a grant search tool?

No. Anansi Atlas is a strategic intelligence layer — not a database you search. It maps the full opportunity landscape around your specific mission: funders, partners, government pathways, readiness gaps, and a 30-day action plan. You don't search it. It builds around you.

What if my organization is small or new?

Anansi Atlas is built for organizations at every stage. The Readiness Score is honest — if you have gaps, it names them and tells you what to address first. Small organizations often benefit most because they don't have research capacity and this replaces what they can't afford to hire.

How long does the intake form take?

About 10 minutes. You'll answer questions about your mission, programs, geography, focus areas, and who you serve. No jargon, no consultants required — just plain language about your work.

When do I get my Opportunity Web Snapshot?

Your Snapshot is included in the founding pilot. Once you're onboarded, we build it around your specific mission and schedule your personal walkthrough call. That's where you see it — together with the Anansi Atlas team, not alone in a dashboard.

What's included in the founding partner price?

$150/month includes full platform access, your Opportunity Web Snapshot, your entire strategic workspace (funding pipeline, readiness tracker, partner map), and a 45-minute personal founder walkthrough call with the team. The price is locked for life — it never increases as long as you stay active.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. No contracts, no commitments. Cancel any time. If you lock in the founding partner rate and leave, the rate isn't guaranteed when you return — but there's no penalty for canceling.

From the founder

"Built by a former nonprofit executive who's been in your shoes."

I spent years working inside the nonprofit sector at the executive level, where I saw how hard mission-driven organizations fight to serve their communities, stretch limited resources, and keep moving forward.

But I also saw something else.

Many organizations were missing opportunities that were already within reach.

Not because they lacked effort.
Not because they lacked passion.
Not because the mission wasn't strong.

The problem was visibility.

Funders were hard to find.
Potential partners were disconnected.
Government pathways were buried.
Resources were scattered.
Readiness gaps quietly blocked progress before an application was ever submitted.

That experience is what led me to build Anansi Atlas.

I wanted to create a clearer way for nonprofits and mission-driven organizations to see the full web of opportunity around their mission — and know where to focus next.

Common Questions

Answers before you decide.

Do founding partners get access to the platform?

Yes. Founding Atlas Partners receive early access to the Anansi Atlas platform, along with a guided Opportunity Web Snapshot and founder-led support.

Is the Snapshot a report or part of the platform?

Both. The Snapshot is the first guided map of your organization's opportunity ecosystem. It can be reviewed as a deliverable, but it is also connected to your organization's platform workspace.

Is Anansi Atlas just a grant search tool?

No. Anansi Atlas looks beyond grants. It helps organize funders, partners, government pathways, community resources, readiness gaps, and next steps around your mission — all in one platform.

What does early access mean?

Early access means your organization gets into the platform during the founding cohort stage while the product is being refined with real nonprofit teams. Founding partners help shape the tools around actual nonprofit needs.

Can we help shape the platform?

Yes. Founding partners receive priority input opportunities so their needs can help shape future features, workflows, and platform improvements. You're not just a customer — you're a co-builder.

What happens after the founding cohort closes?

Founding Atlas Partners keep their $150/month rate locked for life — even as public pricing increases. The next cohort (Early Atlas Partners) opens at $200/month. Public Pro launches at $497/month.

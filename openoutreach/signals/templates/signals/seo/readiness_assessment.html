What Is a Funding Readiness Assessment?

A funding readiness assessment evaluates your organization's current capacity, documentation, partnerships, and track record against the specific requirements of your target funders. It tells you not just who you should pursue — but whether you're ready to pursue them, and what to strengthen before you do.

This is different from a general organizational assessment. It's funder-specific: the gaps that matter for a community foundation are different from those that matter for a federal workforce grant or a corporate funder.

What Readiness Actually Means

When a funder evaluates a grant application, they're looking at far more than your program description. They're assessing:

📊

Outcomes documentation

Can you demonstrate measurable impact? Do you have data on who you served, what changed for them, and how you know? Informal storytelling isn't enough for most funders.

🤝

Partnership relationships

Do you have formal partnerships with organizations whose credibility strengthens your application? Government funders especially want to see established relationships before they invest.

📋

Organizational infrastructure

Financial management systems, board governance, and compliance records. Funders assess organizational health before program quality — a weak infrastructure disqualifies strong programs.

🗺️

Funder relationship history

Do you have prior grants from aligned funders who can serve as references? Cold applications from unknown organizations face a harder road than warm ones.

📝

Program documentation

Is your program model documented clearly enough that a funder can understand what they're funding? Logic models, program summaries, and evaluation frameworks signal organizational maturity.

How the Anansi Atlas Readiness Score Works

Your Readiness Score (0–100) is calculated from your intake responses, cross-referenced against the requirements of your top funder targets. It's not a generic organizational health score — it's calibrated to the specific funders and opportunities most relevant to your mission.

A score of 50–65 typically means strong mission clarity but underdeveloped documentation or partnerships — gaps that are closable in 90 days. A score of 70–85 means you're competitive for most of your top targets, with a few specific areas to strengthen. Above 85, you're well-positioned to pursue your full opportunity landscape aggressively.

Every Readiness Score comes with specific gap analysis — not "improve your outcomes tracking" (vague), but "Document 3 outcome metrics for your workforce program using your existing data — this is the primary gap blocking your DOL eligibility" (actionable).

Why This Matters Before You Apply

A rejected grant application costs you time, staff energy, and sometimes a relationship with a funder you'll want to approach again later. Knowing your readiness gaps before you apply means:

  • You don't waste a proposal on a funder you're not ready for
  • You close the gaps that matter most before your top deadline
  • You sequence your applications so earlier wins strengthen later ones
  • You have a roadmap for organizational development that's tied directly to funding outcomes

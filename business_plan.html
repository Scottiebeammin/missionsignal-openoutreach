01Executive Summary

Anansi Atlas is an opportunity intelligence platform for nonprofits. It maps the full web of opportunity around an organization's mission — aligned funders, strategic partners, active grants with real deadlines, government pathways, and a readiness assessment — and delivers it as an executive-ready Opportunity Web Snapshot plus a living workspace.

The category gap is clear: the 1.8 million US nonprofits are served by tools built for specialists — foundation databases and grant-search platforms that assume a development team and reselling the same licensed lists. Most organizations have neither. Anansi Atlas is built for the executive director, not the grant writer.

Critically, Anansi Atlas does not resell a third-party database. It operates an owned, live data pipeline that ingests verified opportunities from primary sources — the federal Grants.gov API plus grounded web research across local, county, state, national, corporate, and bank funders — with a verification gate that rejects any record without a real source URL. This produces proprietary, defensible data at a fraction of incumbents' cost structure.

02Company & Vision

Anansi Atlas is a product of Scott Foundry Group LLC, founded by Marcus Scott. The name draws on the West African storytelling tradition of Anansi — the clever weaver who sees the hidden connections in the web. That is the company's thesis: the funding landscape is a web, and organizations that can see it move with clarity instead of luck.

Mission: give mission-driven organizations the opportunity intelligence that was always reserved for well-resourced institutions.

Vision: become the intelligence layer for the entire social sector — the system of record for where a nonprofit's next funding, partnership, and pathway will come from.

Why now

  • Primary-source data is finally accessible. Government APIs (Grants.gov, USAspending) and IRS 990 data are open and free — the raw material incumbents aggregate and resell.
  • Grounded AI research can now navigate, extract, and verify funder information at cents-per-organization cost, with a verification gate that eliminates hallucination.
  • The incumbent tools left a gap — built for specialists at large institutions, priced and designed away from the executive directors who need them most.

03The Problem

Nonprofits leave real money on the table — not for lack of merit, but because they cannot see the landscape.

  • No development infrastructure. Most of the 1.8M US nonprofits have no dedicated grant researcher or development director.
  • Research is manual and scattered. Funding intelligence lives across dozens of databases, agency sites, and Google searches — hours of work most leaders cannot spare.
  • Decisions made on luck. Organizations apply to the wrong funders, miss deadlines, and misread their own readiness gaps.
  • Existing tools don't fit. Foundation databases are built for specialists and priced for institutions; they hand over a list, not a strategy.
The core insight
Funding exists; visibility doesn't. The problem is not a people problem — it's a product gap. That makes it a software problem with a software solution.

04The Solution & Product

Anansi Atlas turns a 10-minute intake into a clear, prioritized picture of where an organization should go next — and keeps it live.

The Opportunity Web Snapshot — the core deliverable

A personalized executive intelligence brief, customized to the organization's mission, geography, programs, and stage:

  • Top funder matches — ranked by mission alignment and geographic fit.
  • Active grant opportunities — real deadlines and amounts, ranked by relevance, not dumped as a list.
  • Strategic partners — organizations whose work complements and strengthens applications.
  • Government pathways — federal, state, and local lanes most organizations never see.
  • Readiness Score (0–100) — an honest assessment with specific, named gaps.
  • 30-day action plan — prioritized next steps so leaders know exactly where to start.

The living workspace

Beyond the one-time brief, each organization gets a persistent workspace — snapshot, opportunity pipeline, readiness tracking, and partner intelligence — that stays current as the funding landscape and the organization evolve. Built for executive directors: clean, executive-ready, printable, and shareable.

Built & live today
Intake, deterministic analysis, the Opportunity Web Snapshot, relevance-ranked opportunities (top-10 with full list), the free/low-cost resource directory, readiness scoring, and the operator/CRM back office are all operational in production.

05Technology & Competitive Moat

The defensible advantage is not the interface — it's the owned data pipeline. Competitors license the same aggregated lists; Anansi Atlas builds its data live from primary sources, with every opportunity traceable to a real URL.

Live, owned ingestion

Federal grants pulled directly from the Grants.gov API (free, structured, authoritative). Local, county, state, national, corporate, and bank funders surfaced via grounded web research across government sites, foundation pages, corporate giving programs, and announcements.

The grounding gate

Every researched record must trace to a real, reachable source URL or it is rejected at import. Placeholder and fabricated URLs are blocked. The result: zero hallucinated funders.

Relevance & geography ranking

Each opportunity is scored against what the organization does and who it serves; foreign and out-of-area grants are filtered out unless they fund US-based nonprofits. Leaders see on-mission, eligible matches first.

Cost structure that scales

Primary-source pulls cost cents to ~$1 per organization per refresh — versus the $2,000–$4,300/year per-seat data licenses incumbents resell. Margin compounds as coverage deepens.

Why it compounds
Every research pass improves coverage and the verified funder graph — proprietary data that competitors reselling third-party lists cannot cheaply replicate, and that a nonprofit cannot assemble alone.

Architecture

Self-hosted SaaS (Django, Python) with the data engine modularized into ingestion (grants_gov), grounded research with verification (grounding gate), and relevance scoring (relevance). New orgs are auto-enriched with verified federal grants on intake. Data stays with the organization.

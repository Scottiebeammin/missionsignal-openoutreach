## SCRIPT 1: Demo Video (90–120 seconds)

**[0:00–0:08]**
[SCREEN] anansiatlas.com homepage. Hero headline reads: "The Web of Opportunity — built around your mission." Clean layout. The tagline: "Know what funding exists. Know if you're ready. Know what to do first."
[VO] Most nonprofits spend months chasing funding they're not ready for — or missing the opportunities that actually fit. Anansi Atlas changes that.

**[0:08–0:15]**
[SCREEN] Cursor moves to the "Join Waitlist" button in the nav. Click. Intake form slides in — clean, minimal, 4 sections.
[VO] It starts with a 10-minute intake form. Tell us about your organization — and we'll map the opportunity landscape around you.

**[0:15–0:35]**
[SCREEN] Form being filled out:
- Org name: "Westside Youth Arts Collective"
- Location: Chicago, IL
- Mission (typed in): "We use music, visual arts, and creative job training to support youth of color in building futures on their own terms."
- Focus areas checked: Youth Development, Arts & Culture, Workforce Development
- Populations served checked: Youth, Communities of Color, Low-Income Residents
[VO] We're filling this out for Westside Youth Arts Collective — a Chicago-based org serving youth of color through music, visual arts, and creative job training. Three focus areas. Real populations. Real mission.

**[0:35–0:45]**
[SCREEN] Cursor hits "Submit." Screen transitions to a dark loading state with animated web/spider-graph forming. Text: "Building your Opportunity Web… analyzing 12,000+ funding sources, partner organizations, and federal pathways."
[VO] The platform pulls from thousands of funding sources, active grant cycles, and federal pathways — and maps what's relevant to this specific organization.

**[0:45–1:05]**
[SCREEN] Snapshot appears. Clean dashboard layout. Walk through each section:
- Mission overview card: confirms org focus, populations, geography
- Funder Archetypes (3 tiles): Community Foundations (Chicago Community Trust, Polk Bros. Foundation), Corporate Funders (arts/workforce alignment), Federal Grants (NEA, DOL Youth Workforce)
- Active Grants with deadlines: 3 grants listed — e.g., "NEA Grants for Arts Projects — Deadline: Aug 15" / "Polk Bros. Foundation — Rolling deadline"
- Readiness Score: 72/100 — gauge animates to 72. Tag: "Strong foundation. A few gaps to close before your top grant targets."
[VO] Here's the Opportunity Web Snapshot. Westside Youth Arts gets their top funder archetypes — community foundations, corporate funders aligned to creative workforce, and federal arts and workforce grants. Three active grants with real deadlines. And a Readiness Score of 72 out of 100 — strong, but with specific gaps to close before their top targets.

**[1:05–1:15]**
[SCREEN] 30-Day Action Plan section expands. Numbered list:
1. Formalize your workforce outcomes framework (required for DOL eligibility)
2. Draft one-page program summary aligned to NEA language
3. Identify a co-applicant partner for federal submissions
4. Request intro meeting with a Polk Bros. program officer
[VO] Then there's the 30-day action plan. Not a wish list — specific steps, sequenced by impact, built around where they actually are today.

**[1:15–1:25]**
[SCREEN] Transition to the Opportunity Web visualization — an interactive graph. The org sits at the center. Lines radiate out to funder nodes (blue), partner nodes (green), and government pathway nodes (orange). Hovering a funder node shows name, grant range, deadline.
[VO] And this is the web — a live map of every relevant opportunity, who connects to who, and where to start pulling threads.

**[1:25–1:35]**
[SCREEN] Pull back to the full Snapshot. CTA banner at the bottom: "19 founding partner seats remaining — $150/month. Join the waitlist at anansiatlas.com."
[VO] Anansi Atlas isn't a grant-writing tool. It's strategic intelligence — clarity on what exists, what fits, and what to do first. Nineteen founding partner seats left at $150 a month. Join the waitlist at anansiatlas.com.

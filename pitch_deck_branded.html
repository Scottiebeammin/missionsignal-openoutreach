The Problem

Nonprofits leave real money on the table — not for lack of merit, but because they can't see the landscape.

  • 1.8 million US nonprofits — most with no dedicated development staff or grant researcher.
  • Grant research is manual, slow, and scattered across dozens of databases and Google searches.
  • Organizations apply to the wrong funders, miss deadlines, and misread their own readiness gaps.
  • The result: underfunded missions, burned-out staff, and opportunities that never get pursued.

Funding exists. Visibility doesn't. Most nonprofits make grant decisions on luck — and luck doesn't scale.

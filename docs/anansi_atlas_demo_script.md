## Recording Goal

Show Anansi Atlas as an Opportunity Command Center for nonprofit leaders: a place to see readiness, relationships, pathways, evidence, and next actions without reading a long report.

## 60-Second Demo Script

Anansi Atlas helps mission-driven organizations see and manage their Web of Opportunity.

Instead of scattering funding leads, government relationships, partner conversations, documents, evidence, and deadlines across separate tools, Anansi Atlas brings them into one executive command center.

Here, BridgeForward Digital Futures can see readiness, forecast value, relationship health, pathway health, upcoming deadlines, and the highest-leverage next action in one view.

The Opportunity Web shows how the mission connects to funders, partners, contacts, resources, opportunities, and outcomes.

The Snapshot turns that intelligence into a premium executive summary that can guide a board conversation, funder meeting, or pilot review.

From there, the team can move into Pathways and Pipeline to decide what to pursue, what is ready, what is blocked, and who should act next.

Anansi Atlas is built for organizations that do not just need more opportunities. They need to understand which opportunities are worth pursuing and what would make them more ready to win.

## 2-Minute Demo Script

Anansi Atlas is an Opportunity Command Center for nonprofits, workforce organizations, foundations, and consultants.

Most organizations do not have a shortage of activity. They have scattered signals: funding leads in one place, government contacts in another, partner relationships in someone else's notes, deadlines in spreadsheets, and readiness gaps that only appear when an application is already due.

Anansi Atlas organizes those signals into the Web of Opportunity.

The public page introduces the core promise: helping organizations move from fragmented opportunity work to a clearer, more fundable, more actionable strategy.

Inside the dashboard, leadership can see the current state in seconds: readiness, forecast value, relationship health, pathway health, upcoming deadlines, what to do next, and recent wins across the web.

The Start Here flow gives a natural demo path. First, open the Opportunity Web. This view shows the organization as an ecosystem: mission, funders, partners, contacts, resources, opportunities, and outcomes.

Next, open the Opportunity Web Snapshot. This is the premium executive takeaway: a concise consulting-style view of the organization's opportunity position.

Then move into Pathways. This is where discovered opportunities become choices. The system shows match health, readiness, priority, deadlines, and pursuit context so the team can decide what deserves attention.

The Pipeline board turns that intelligence into execution. Opportunities move from discovered to reviewing, qualified, pursuing, drafting, submitted, awarded, declined, or closed. Each card keeps the next step, owner, readiness, tasks, deadlines, and forecast context close at hand.

Readiness explains what is missing before a pursuit becomes urgent: outcomes, documents, evidence, budget details, partner letters, or relationship gaps.

Relationships show the human side of the web: funders, partners, government contacts, and trusted connectors that can change the odds.

The point is simple: Anansi Atlas does not just list opportunities. It helps an organization understand its position, strengthen its readiness, and manage the work required to pursue the right opportunities.

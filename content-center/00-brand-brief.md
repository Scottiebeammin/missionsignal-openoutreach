## What it is
Anansi Atlas is a **nonprofit opportunity intelligence platform** that helps nonprofits and mission-driven organizations **map the web of opportunity around their mission**. It is NOT "just grant search," not a grant-writing agency, not a guaranteed-funding solution, not a charity.

**Core offer / deliverable:** the **Opportunity Web Snapshot**.

**What it helps organizations identify:**
aligned funders · potential partners · government pathways · community resources · readiness gaps · strategic risks · routes to capital · practical next steps

**Product node vocabulary (use these exact category names):**
Funders · Partners · Government · Resources · Readiness · Pathways

## Positioning (the spine of every message)
Nonprofits are surrounded by opportunity, but it's scattered across websites, funder portals, emails, spreadsheets, relationships, local knowledge, and institutional memory. Anansi Atlas helps organizations **see the opportunity landscape around their mission clearly — so they move from scattered opportunity to focused action.**

## The campaign (right now)
- **Goal:** recruit **19–20 nonprofit / mission-driven organizations** into the **Founding Atlas Partners Pilot**.
- **Pricing (real, live):** Founding rate **$150/month, locked for life** for the first 20 orgs; **$299/month** standard after the cohort. No per-report fees, no usage limits, cancel anytime.
- **The Snapshot is NOT a free lead magnet.** It is **included in the subscription** — you join the pilot at $150/mo and the Snapshot + 45-minute walkthrough + platform access come with it. Never advertise a "free Snapshot."

## Production standards
- **Voiceover / narration: use ElevenLabs** for all video VO. Pick one consistent, credible, warm-but-serious voice and reuse it across every video so the brand has a recognizable sound. Match the pacing in each script's timestamps; keep delivery calm and confident (not hype-y announcer). Export narration, then cut picture to it.
- **House voice: a custom ElevenLabs VOICE LIBRARY voice (NOT a premade default).** Picking from the Voice Library so the brand doesn't sound like every other ElevenLabs project (and stays distinct from Myths & Midnight / Joey Patel).
  - **Voice-choice logic (from sales/VO research):** gender does NOT reliably "sell more" — *lower pitch* (male or female) reads as more competent/trustworthy, and *fit with brand + audience* beats gender. Our audience (nonprofit leaders) skews ~2/3 female, and our tone is warm + credible. So a **warm, lower-pitched, middle-aged American voice** is the target; a warm female voice is an especially on-audience choice, a calm authoritative male is equally defensible. Choose on trust + fit, not gender.
  - **Voice Library filter recipe:** Use case = Narration/Informative/Advertising · Gender = try Female first, compare a male · Age = Middle-aged · Accent = American · search tone words "warm / confident / professional / trustworthy / documentary" · AVOID "casual / hype / energetic / soft / ASMR / sleepy."

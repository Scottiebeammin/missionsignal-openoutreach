**1. Scattered opportunity**
Most nonprofit leaders I talk to aren't short on opportunity. They're short on a way to see it.

Aligned funders. Potential partners. Government pathways. Community resources. It's all out there — scattered across portals, inboxes, spreadsheets, and someone's institutional memory.

That's the Opportunity Web Snapshot. It leads with a plain-English summary and a 30-day action plan, then maps the pathways underneath.

**3. Web of Opportunity (education)**
A grant database gives you a list. That's not the same as a strategy.

Your mission sits at the center of a web — funders on one strand, partners on another, government pathways, community resources, and your own readiness holding it together. The value isn't the list. It's seeing how the strands connect.

Anansi Atlas maps that web. Founding pilot is open now.

**4. Readiness / risks**
Hard truth: the fastest way to lose a funding opportunity is to chase one you're not ready for.

Readiness gaps and strategic risks are the quiet reasons strong missions stall. So we built them into the Snapshot — not as a scolding, but as a map of what to shore up before you pitch.

Clarity before capital. That's the whole idea. Pilot details in the comments.

**5. Funding / partnership clarity**
"Where should we focus this quarter?" is the hardest question in nonprofit leadership.

Not because there are no answers — because there are too many, and no clean way to compare them.

Anansi Atlas turns that fog into a ranked, visual map: which funders align, which partners are worth a conversation, which pathways are actually open to you right now. Founding Atlas Partners Pilot is live.

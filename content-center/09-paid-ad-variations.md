## 1. AUDIENCE & TARGETING

### LinkedIn (primary — precise, intent-rich, expensive)

**Job titles (OR-group):**
- Executive Director · CEO / President (nonprofit) · Chief Executive
- Development Director · Director of Development · VP of Advancement · Chief Development Officer
- Grants Manager · Director of Grants · Grant Writer · Foundation Relations Manager
- Founder · Co-Founder · Executive Director & Founder
- Director of Programs · Director of Operations (secondary, lower bid)

**Member skills (layer to catch mis-titled leaders):** Grant Writing · Fundraising · Nonprofit Management · Program Development · Philanthropy · Grant Administration · Nonprofit Organizations.

**Company / industry:** Nonprofit Organization Management · Civic & Social Organization · Philanthropy · Individual & Family Services · Higher Education (mission-aligned) · Religious Institutions (optional). Use LinkedIn's **Nonprofit** industry cluster.

**Company size:** 1–10, 11–50, 51–200 employees. **Exclude 501+** (enterprise nonprofits have in-house development teams and RFP procurement — wrong buyer for a $150/mo self-serve pilot).

**Geography:** United States (primary). Optional Tier-2 layer: Canada. If budget is tight, narrow to states with dense nonprofit sectors first (CA, NY, TX, IL, MA, WA, DC-metro, GA, FL).

**Seniority (as a sanity layer, not primary):** Director, VP, CXO, Owner, Partner.

**EXCLUDE on LinkedIn:**
- Company size **501+** employees.
- Job functions: **Sales, Business Development, Marketing** at software companies (they'll click to study the funnel, not buy).
- Titles containing **"Consultant / Agency / Freelance grant writer"** unless running a warm remarketing test — they may resell, not adopt.
- **Students, interns, "seeking opportunities"** (job-seeker intent).
- Anyone already in the CRM as a **closed founding partner** (suppress via matched-audience upload once seats fill).
- Grant-management **software competitors' employees** (Instrumentl, Submittable, Fluxx) via company exclusion — avoid tipping the hand and wasting spend.

**Remarketing / warm audiences (highest ROI — build from day one):**
- Website visitors to `anansiatlas.com` (all + signup-page-specific).
- Video viewers (25% / 50% / 75%) from the walkthrough and Snapshot feature videos.
- LinkedIn Page + post engagers (last 365 days).
- Lead-gen form openers who didn't submit.

### Meta (Instagram / Facebook — broader, looser, cheaper; proxy-target by interest)

Meta cannot target "Development Director" directly, so target the **interests, behaviors, and affinities nonprofit leaders cluster around**, then let the creative self-select (the ad copy names the buyer: "If you run a nonprofit…").

**Core interests (OR):** Nonprofit organization · Fundraising · Grant (money) · Foundation (nonprofit) · Philanthropy · Volunteering · Social entrepreneurship · Charitable organization · GuideStar / Candid · Chronicle of Philanthropy · Nonprofit Quarterly.

**Behaviors / affinities:** Small business owners · Admins of a nonprofit/charity Facebook Page (strong proxy) · Nonprofit tech users (Bloomerang, DonorPerfect, Neon CRM, Classy, GiveButter, Instrumentl fans) · attendees/followers of AFP (Association of Fundraising Professionals), Nonprofit Tech for Good.

**Publications / orgs to layer:** Candid, TechSoup, Foundation Center, Idealist, The Chronicle of Philanthropy, Stanford Social Innovation Review.

**Demographics:** Age 30–64 · US · optionally "Job title: Executive Director / Founder / Fundraiser" where available in detailed targeting (spotty, use as bonus layer not sole gate).

**Placements:** Start Instagram Feed + Stories + Facebook Feed. Add Reels for video variants. Turn off Audience Network for cold (junk clicks).

**Lookalikes (once data exists):** 1% LAL of website converters + LAL of email list of nonprofit contacts / founding applicants.

**EXCLUDE on Meta:**
- **Under 30** (rarely the budget holder).
- Interest **"Work from home / make money online / MLM"** overlap (attracts opportunity-seekers, not nonprofit ops).
- Broad **"charity donor"**-only audiences with no professional/admin signal (that's a *donor*, not a nonprofit operator — wrong side of the table).
- Existing converters and current founding partners (suppress).
- Audience Network placement for cold traffic.

**Reality check for both platforms:** category is unknown, so **cold audiences convert on education, not urgency**. Lead urgency ("19 seats") with warm/remarketing audiences; lead with the *problem + product* on cold.

## 5. A/B TEST MATRIX

> Rule: change **one variable per test**, keep everything else fixed, hold the audience constant. LinkedIn needs patience (higher CPMs, fewer impressions) — give each test enough spend to reach ~100+ clicks or a handful of applications before judging.

| # | Test (variable) | Variant A | Variant B | Hypothesis | Primary metric | Secondary |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| T1 | **Hook angle** (cold LI) | AD 1 — Pain ("scattered tabs") | AD 2 — Product ("not a grant list, a map") | For an unaware category, PAIN earns the click but PRODUCT education drives quality. | CTR (click) | Cost / landing-page view, apply-start rate |
| T2 | **Outcome vs. pain** (cold LI) | AD 3 — 30-day plan (outcome) | AD 5 — Readiness (pain/credibility) | Outcome-clarity converts warmer; honest-mirror builds trust & lower-funnel intent. | Landing-page view → apply-start | Time on page |
| T3 | **Creative type** (LI) | AD 6 — 60s walkthrough video | CAROUSEL B — Snapshot document | Video proves "it's real" cheaply; carousel drives deeper self-education. | Cost per apply-start | 25/50/75% video view, carousel completion |
| T4 | **Headline** (same body, AD 2) | "Not a grant list. A map of your opportunity web." | "Opportunity intelligence for your mission" | Concrete contrast ("not a list") beats abstract category label on a cold feed. | CTR | Apply-start rate |
| T5 | **CTA button** (same ad) | Learn more | Apply now | "Learn more" wins cold (low commitment); "Apply now" wins warm (ready intent). | CTR + apply-start | Cost per application |
| T6 | **Scarcity vs. education** (WARM/retarget) | AD 4 — "19 seats" scarcity | CAROUSEL C — offer, no fine print | On warm traffic, explicit scarcity out-converts a calm offer recap. | Applications | Cost per application |
| T7 | **Platform / audience** | LinkedIn precise-title cold | Meta interest-proxy cold (META 1/2) | LinkedIn = higher CPC, higher quality; Meta = cheaper reach, more education needed. | Cost per *qualified* apply | Volume of apply-starts |
| T8 | **Meta hook** (cold) | META 1 — pain | META 6 — emotional/mission | Emotional-mission stops the scroll better in a consumer feed than problem-framing. | Thumb-stop rate / CTR | Cost per landing-page view |

### Sequential test plan (small pre-launch budget)

**Phase 0 — Foundations (before spending):** install tracking (see §6), stand up the retargeting pixels/insight tag, confirm the signup page loads fast and the apply flow works on mobile.

**Phase 1 — Angle (cold, LinkedIn) → run T1 + T2.** Find the winning *hook angle* first; it's the single biggest lever. Keep audience = LinkedIn precise-title cold. ~5–7 days.

**Phase 2 — Format (LinkedIn) → run T3** with the Phase-1 winning angle baked into both video and carousel. Learn whether video or document earns cheaper apply-starts.

**Phase 3 — Cheap reach test → run T7 (+ T8)** on Meta with the winning angle adapted. Meta gives cheap volume to pressure-test whether the message educates a broader audience at all.

**Phase 4 — Conversion polish → run T4, T5** (headline + CTA micro-tests) on the winning creative to squeeze CTR / apply-start.

**Phase 5 — Warm close → run T6** on the retargeting pool you've now built. This is where scarcity/offer ads finally run and where most applications should land.

### Rough budget guidance (founder testing cheaply)

- **Total starter:** **$1,500–$2,500 over 3–4 weeks** is enough to learn without burning cash. If tighter, **$750–$1,000** run slower.
- **Split:** ~**65% LinkedIn** (quality buyer, primary channel), ~**25% Meta** (cheap reach + retargeting engine), ~**10% reserve** for scaling the winner.
- **Per test:** budget each A/B cell to reach **~100 clicks or ~1,000–1,500 impressions minimum** before calling it; on LinkedIn plan **$25–$40/day per campaign** (LI has ~$10/day floors and high CPCs — expect **$8–$14 CPC**, **$6–$10 CPM** ranges vary).
- **Meta:** **$10–$20/day per ad set** cold; CPCs far lower (~$0.70–$2.50). Use it to *build the retargeting audience* even before it converts directly.
- **Don't judge applications on cold spend alone** — cold educates, warm converts. Watch **cost per apply-start** and **cost per landing-page view** early; **cost per application** becomes reliable only once the retargeting pool is warm (Phase 5).
- **Kill rule:** pause any cell with a CTR under ~0.4% (LI) after ~1,500 impressions or clearly worst-in-test cost per apply-start; **reallocate to the leader**, don't add fresh budget to laggards.
